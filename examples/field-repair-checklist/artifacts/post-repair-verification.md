## Purpose

Verify that the repair was successful and the equipment is safe to return to service before handing off to the operator.

## Step 1: Assembly and Closure Verification

1. Confirm all panels and covers are properly closed and secured.
2. Confirm no tools or materials are left inside or near the equipment.
3. Confirm all cable connections are re-seated if disconnected during repair.

## Step 2: Power Restoration and Functional Test

1. Restore power supply and confirm no fault indicators appear on startup.
2. Allow the equipment to complete its startup sequence.
3. Perform a brief functional test of the repaired system:
   - If a motor was replaced: verify it runs in the correct direction at expected speed.
   - If a control board was replaced: verify all indicators show nominal state.
   - If a mechanical linkage was repaired: verify smooth motion through the full range.

## Step 3: Escalation Check

If any of the following are observed after repair, do not return equipment to service:

- New error codes or indicators that were not present before the repair
- Unusual noise, heat, or smell during the functional test
- Mechanical motion that is rough, binding, or erratic

Escalate to level-2 support with the documented findings.

## Step 4: Operator Handoff

1. Notify the operator or supervisor that maintenance is complete.
2. Describe the fault found and the repair performed.
3. Note any remaining items requiring monitoring or follow-up.
4. Obtain operator signature on the work order if required by your organization.

## Step 5: Documentation

Close out the work order with:

- Date and time of completion
- Repair performed
- Parts replaced (part numbers if applicable)
- Functional test result
- Technician name
