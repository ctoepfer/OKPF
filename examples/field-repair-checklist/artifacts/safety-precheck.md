## Purpose

This fictional precheck ensures that basic safety conditions are confirmed before beginning any repair activity on general light machinery in the example domain.

## Before You Begin

1. **Confirm work order.** Verify that a documented work order exists and that the equipment is correctly identified by serial number or asset tag.
2. **Notify the operator.** Inform the equipment operator or supervisor that maintenance activity will begin. Confirm the equipment is not in active use.
3. **Gather tools and PPE.** Collect required personal protective equipment (PPE) for the task. At minimum: safety glasses and work gloves.

## Power and Energy Isolation

1. Power off the equipment using the designated power switch or control panel.
2. Unplug the power supply from the wall outlet or lock out the circuit breaker (LOTO procedure as applicable to your context).
3. Wait at least 60 seconds after power-off before touching internal components. This allows capacitors to discharge in applicable equipment categories.
4. Confirm equipment is de-energized before proceeding.

## Physical Inspection Before Opening

1. Inspect the equipment exterior for visible damage: cracked casing, frayed cables, burn marks, or leaking fluids.
2. If visible damage is found, do not proceed with self-service repair. Escalate to the equipment manufacturer or a qualified technician.
3. Confirm work area is clear of tripping hazards and has adequate lighting.

## Documentation

Record the following before starting repair:

- Date and time
- Asset ID or serial number
- Fault description as reported by operator
- Name of technician performing the precheck
