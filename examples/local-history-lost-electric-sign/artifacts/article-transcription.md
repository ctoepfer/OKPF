## Transcribed text

Electric Sign

Community Decides to Erect Sign on Bonita at San Dimas Ave.

SAN DIMAS — The electric sign, which for the past year has been under controversy, is to be erected immediately at the corner of San Dimas avenue and Bonita, it was announced today, following action taken at the chamber of commerce meeting last night. The sign, which was donated to the community by William McNeil of Charter Oak, is a large illuminated rectangle bearing the name of the city in large letters.

John Campbell and L. R. Belknap were appointed on the committee to attend to the installation, and reported last night that the electricity would cost the chamber of commerce approximately $10 each month.

The position of the sign was discussed, with a possibility of placing it diagonally so that it might be seen from both streets.
