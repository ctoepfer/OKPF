# Location Analysis

## Reported location

The article states the sign was to be erected "at the corner of San Dimas
avenue and Bonita" — i.e., the corner of **San Dimas Avenue and Bonita
Avenue** in San Dimas, California. This is the only location information
given in the source.

## Orientation: an open question, not a decision

The article states that the sign's position "was discussed, with a
possibility of placing it diagonally so that it might be seen from both
streets." This is reported as a discussion of a possibility at the time of
publication — not a confirmed final placement. This pack does not treat
"diagonal placement" as a settled fact; see
[`records/claims.jsonl`](../records/claims.jsonl) (claim
`claim-diagonal-placement-possibility`), which records this at lower
confidence than the more directly reported facts.

## What this analysis does not do

This pack does not assign or invent:

- Exact coordinates, lot numbers, or parcel references for the corner.
- A confirmed final orientation (diagonal or otherwise).
- Any correlation to a present-day address, since street layouts and
  naming conventions can change over nearly a century.

None of that information is present in the source article, and none of it
should be inferred without independent research. A future researcher
extending this pack could add such detail as new, separately-sourced
records — see [`records/research-leads.jsonl`](../records/research-leads.jsonl)
for candidate next steps (e.g., historical maps, permit records).
