# Research Summary — The Bonita Avenue Electric Sign

This pack preserves a single primary source — a 1927 newspaper article — and
the structured claims, people, timeline, and open questions that can
reasonably be drawn from it. It does not claim to be a complete history of
the sign.

## What is source-attested

The following statements are directly supported by the transcribed article
(see [`article-transcription.md`](article-transcription.md) and
[`records/claims.jsonl`](../records/claims.jsonl) for the structured form):

- The chamber of commerce, at a meeting reported as occurring the night
  before publication, took action to have an electric sign "erected
  immediately" at the corner of San Dimas Avenue and Bonita Avenue in San
  Dimas, California.
- The sign had reportedly been "under controversy" for about a year before
  this announcement.
- The sign was donated to the community by William McNeil of Charter Oak.
- The sign was described as a large illuminated rectangle bearing the
  city's name in large letters.
- John Campbell and L. R. Belknap were appointed to a committee to attend
  to the sign's installation.
- The committee reported an estimated electricity cost of approximately
  $10 per month to the chamber of commerce.
- The article discussed the possibility of placing the sign diagonally at
  the corner so it could be seen from both streets — described as a
  possibility under discussion, not a final decision.

## What remains unknown

This pack does not contain, and does not claim to contain, evidence that
resolves:

- Whether the sign was actually installed following this announcement.
- The sign's final, confirmed placement or orientation (diagonal or
  otherwise).
- Who owned or maintained the sign after installation, if it occurred.
- Whether the chamber of commerce paid ongoing electricity costs.
- Whether any photographs, postcards, or other visual records of the sign
  exist.
- When, why, or how the sign was removed, damaged, replaced, or forgotten.

See [`unanswered-questions.md`](unanswered-questions.md) for the full list
of open questions and [`records/research-leads.jsonl`](../records/research-leads.jsonl)
for where a future researcher might look next.

## What This Pack Does Not Claim

This pack does not prove:

- that the sign was actually installed
- how long the sign remained
- that no photos exist
- when or why the sign disappeared
- that the article is the only source

An absence of evidence for these points in this pack is not evidence that
they didn't happen — it means they have not yet been researched here. See
[`docs/historical-evidence-packs.md`](../../../docs/historical-evidence-packs.md)
for why OKPF historical packs are built to preserve that distinction
explicitly, rather than resolve it by omission.
