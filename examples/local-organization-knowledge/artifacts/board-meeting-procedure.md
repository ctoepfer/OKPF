## Purpose

This procedure defines the standard process for scheduling, conducting, and documenting board meetings for the Example Community Organization.

## Scheduling

1. The secretary schedules meetings at least 14 days in advance.
2. Meetings are held on the first Tuesday of each month unless otherwise agreed.
3. Notice is sent to all board members via the organization's internal mailing list.
4. Agenda items are collected from board members no later than five days before the meeting.

## Quorum

A quorum is reached when at least half of the seated board members are present.

Decisions require a simple majority of members present unless the bylaws specify otherwise for a particular matter.

## Agenda

The agenda is circulated at least 48 hours before the meeting. Standard agenda sections:

1. Call to order
2. Approval of previous minutes
3. Treasurer's report
4. Old business
5. New business
6. Announcements
7. Adjournment

## Minutes

Minutes are recorded by the secretary or a designated note-taker. Minutes include:

- Date, time, location, and attendees
- Summary of each agenda item and its outcome
- All motions, seconders, and vote results
- Action items with assigned owners and due dates

Draft minutes are circulated within five business days. Final minutes are approved at the next meeting.

## Records Retention

Approved minutes are retained for seven years in the organization's document repository.
